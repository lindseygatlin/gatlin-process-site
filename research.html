Initial Client Research

Who is Loaves and Fishes? Why does their work mattter?

"Loaves & Fishes provides a week’s worth of nutritionally balanced groceries to individuals and families experiencing a short-term crisis through a network of 41 emergency food pantries located throughout Mecklenburg County."

In the state of North Carolina, 1 in 7 individuals struggle with hunger and 1 in 5 children struggle. It's also been found that eople facing hunger in North Carolina are estimated to report needing $719,879,000 more per year to meet their food needs (Feeding America). In 2020, Loaves and Fishes provided food to over 120,200 people in Mecklenburg County (a county of around 1 million). They partner with the Second Harvest Food Bank of Metrolina, a large and sucessful food bank in the area.

Currently, their site is a bit inconsistent with the typefaces and iconography. It is also challenging/time-consuming to navigate. The inconsistencies magnify the navigational difficulities. The buttons on the site also vary greatly which makes navigation even greater of a challenge. The navigation menus are also problematic, and some even cut off the words.

Loaves and Fishes' current branding could also use a referesh. The symbol feels outdated and even unprofessional. Updating their branding will allow the site to have a more cohesive structure.

Secondary Research

A Look at the Competition

  1. Explore the most recent news in your client's field. Use the Internet, newspapers, magazines, or journals to know what’s new. This can be news stories of interest, relevant scientific research, or anything that could help you in finding a solution to the design challenge.

    Feeding America, a large national organization, found that America has more than enough food to feed everyone but, much of it is wasted. They estimate that "...25–40% of food grown, processed and transported in the U.S. will never be consumed...". They also stated that food is the most wasted thing in the country. End Hunger in America, another large organization, opens their site by saying: "Hunger is a foundational problem – without addressing it, many of the other challenges faced by our society, from education to employment to poverty, are simply insoluble." Hunger is a major issue but, the issue of quality food matters as well. Feeding America also explains that "...healthy variety of foods, such as fresh fruits and vegetables, whole grains, low-fat dairy and lean protein..." is needed to truly make a difference. Doing the drive thrus seem to be more successful than the tradtional pantries in finding volunteers to support them.

  2. Try to find recent innovations in your client's particular area. They could be technological, behavioral, or cultural.

    3. For my organization, the COVID-19 pandemic presented them with new challenges in getting what they had to those in need. One solution they found to be sucessful are their Pop Up Food Shares. These events allow participants to pre-reigster online and stop by a central location to drive thru and receieve fresh produce, meat, and dairy. Attending a Pop Up event like this does not count in the pantry visits each indivual is alloted per calendar year—allowing them a chance to be provided with more. They have also switched their normal pantry operations to be drive thru style as well.

  3. What are some possible solutions to your problem?

    4. It could be important to create a digital space where information about and directions to mobile pantry sites would be beneficial. It would also be helpful to consider ways to ensure that pantry sites are as sustainable as possible to not contribute to the astonishing amount of wasted food. A focus on the mobile experience will also be important.

  4. Take a look at other solutions in your area. Which ones worked? Which ones didn’t? Are there any that feel similar to what you might design? Any solutions that have inspired you to make one of your own?

    5. There do not seem to be any similar solutions in the Charlotte area. The other major supplier (and partner of Loaves and Fishes) is Second Harvest Food Bank; but, their site is very volunteer directed and finding how to recieve food is more challenging. It is also hard to use on a mobile device which can be more accesible than a laptop or desktop computer.

  5. Is there any market data that you can dig up that may be relevant in helping you solve the design challenge?

    6. 1 in 7 individuals in NC not knowing where they will get their next meal suggests that something is not working. Especially with COVID-19, websites matter more than before. Devoting time to this site could help others get the assitance they need.
